Key Findings

1. Distributed structure is universal
Every model tested — Qwen, MiniCPM-o, Qwen2-VL — shows PC1:PC2 < 2:1 at the personality-relevant depth (3/4 layers). The "semantic nebula" is not an artifact of one architecture. It's how transformers organize high-level concepts.
2. MiniCPM-o: extreme channel efficiency
MiniCPM-o-4.5's omni-modal architecture concentrates information flow along a highly efficient primary manifold channel. Where other models spread variance across many dimensions, MiniCPM-o routes it through fewer, more powerful channels — achieving high throughput with concentrated geometry. This aligns with density-law predictions for optimized architectures.
3. Vision alignment reshapes deep layers
Qwen2-VL retains higher concentration at depth (6.2:1 at L27) compared to text-only Qwen2.5-Instruct (2.5:1). Visual grounding appears to impose a structural constraint that persists even in the text-only representation pathway. This has implications for Joi: VLM personality envelopes may have different shapes.
4. RLHF smooths the deep manifold
Base → Instruct comparison: deep-layer PC1:PC2 drops from 5.3:1 to 2.5:1. Alignment doesn't just remove cliffs — it actively distributes the representation across more dimensions. This is why aligned models have larger safe envelopes (94% vs 1.3%) and why they resist personality steering.
